Common Tick-Related Problems and Signs You Need Tick Control

Understanding the signs of a potential tick problem is the first step in protecting your property and loved ones in Casa Grande. Ignoring a tick problem can have serious consequences, as ticks can reproduce rapidly and spread dangerous diseases.

Discovering Ticks on Pets

One of the most frequent ways homeowners discover a tick issue is by finding ticks on their dogs or cats. Pets that spend time outdoors are particularly vulnerable to picking up ticks. If you're regularly finding ticks attached to your pet, or if they are exhibiting symptoms like excessive scratching, head shaking, or behavioral changes, it's a strong indication that ticks are active on your property. Ticks prefer to attach to areas with less fur, such as the ears, neck, head, and legs of your pet. Checking your pets regularly, especially after they've been in tall grass or wooded areas, is a good preventative measure, but it's not always enough to identify the full extent of the problem on your property. Finding multiple ticks on your pet suggests a significant tick population nearby that needs professional attention. Our residential pest control services are designed to keep your home and pets safe.

Tick Bites on Humans

Finding a tick attached to yourself or a family member immediately raises concerns about potential disease transmission. While not all ticks carry diseases, the risk exists, and it's a clear sign that ticks are present on your property and potentially posing a health hazard. Tick bites can sometimes be accompanied by a rash, and symptoms of tick-borne illnesses can vary widely. If you or someone in your household finds a tick attached, it's important to remove it properly and monitor for any symptoms. The presence of tick bites on humans is a strong indicator that you need professional tick control services to reduce the tick population and minimize the risk of future bites.
